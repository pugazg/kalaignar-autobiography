# 3975. An Ocean of Announcements; Not Even a Mustard Seed Fulfilled!

## Translator's Note

This translation is intended to carry Kalaignar's voice into clear, contemporary English rather than recast the letter as literary or academic prose. It preserves the source's argument, political directness, rhetorical questions, repetition, irony, factual detail, and paragraph order. Names, dates, figures, quotations, and intentional English expressions are retained. Where Tamil idiom cannot be reproduced literally without sounding unnatural, the English follows its sense and rhetorical force without adding claims absent from the source. The original Tamil is reproduced in full below the translation and remains the authoritative text.

The Tamil section below is a corrected reading copy; the canonical Tamil chapter remains untouched. All twelve source pages were checked for dates, costs, project counts, percentages, and page joins. Visual inspection restored the 7-5-2012 date and the OCR-lost passage on the ₹113.26-crore Amma Farm Women's Skill Development Scheme. Printed figures are preserved even where independent arithmetic might suggest a different result.

## English Translation

My dear brethren,

The Legislative Assembly has begun. From now on, Chief Minister Jayalalithaa will begin reading statements every day under Assembly Rule 110. What does Rule 110 of the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly say? Only this: “A Minister may make a statement on a matter of public importance.” But twisting the words “a Minister” to mean “the Chief Minister alone,” she has denied every departmental Minister an opportunity over the past four years. The Chief Minister alone has read every statement under Rule 110 in the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly. In that case, what became of the schemes announced in the statements Jayalalithaa read under Rule 110 during those four years? Were they completed, or did they remain only on paper?

Between 8 June and 13 September 2011, Chief Minister Jayalalithaa read nineteen statements under Rule 110. Those statements contained fifty-four announcements.

Between 29 March and 2 November 2012, she read sixty-one statements containing ninety-nine announcements. Between 1 April and 15 May 2013, she read forty-six statements containing 189 announcements.

Between 14 July and 12 August 2014, the Chief Minister read thirty-nine statements containing 235 announcements.

Thus, over four years, she read a total of 165 statements under Rule 110. Because each statement contained more than one announcement, Chief Minister Jayalalithaa made a total of 577 announcements in those 165 statements. How many of the schemes announced in them have been completed in full? How many have stopped halfway? How many have not even begun, and why? The AIADMK Government must explain this to the people; that is the Government's duty. When O. Panneerselvam was Chief Minister in the Assembly, he said Jayalalithaa had announced 235 schemes under Rule 110 in 2013-14 and that Government Orders had been issued for 116 of them. Even then, I asked why no orders had been issued for the remaining 120 schemes.

From the information available to me, I shall now set out which schemes announced by Chief Minister Jayalalithaa under Rule 110 remain unfulfilled.

In a statement read on 7 September 2011, Jayalalithaa said: “I am happy to announce that the 113-kilometre stretch of road from Thoothukudi to Anjugramam in Kanyakumari district will be widened into a two-lane road and improved at an estimated cost of ₹257 crore.” Of that ₹257 crore, only ₹17.40 crore had reportedly been spent. What reason do they give for a delay of more than three years? Land still has to be acquired, it seems.

That same day, she announced that twenty-three new railway overbridges and underpasses would be constructed in nine districts at a cost of ₹740 crore. Do you know how much of that ₹740 crore had been spent by 15 December 2014? Just ₹10 crore. Of those twenty-three works, land acquisition was said to be underway for eight, while detailed project reports were being prepared for seven. No one knows what became of the remaining eight.

On 8 September 2011, Jayalalithaa announced that the Tamil Nadu Housing Board would establish the “Thirumazhisai Satellite Township” at a cost of ₹2,160 crore. Four years have passed since the announcement. Where is the satellite township? A Government Order for the scheme announced on 8 September 2011 was issued only on 5 March 2012, six months later! They announced ₹2,160 crore. Do you know how much they had spent by 15 December 2014? Only ₹38.77 crore! Is that true or false?

In the same statement, Jayalalithaa said that 3.73 acres owned by the Tamil Nadu Housing Board near Ashok Pillar in Chennai had remained unused, and that, considering Chennai's housing needs, apartment blocks containing 554 homes would be built there at an estimated cost of ₹100 crore. How many homes have been built? How many crores have been spent? Not even one crore. A Government Order was issued six months after the announcement, and the project proposal was still said to be under preparation!

On 8 September 2011, Jayalalithaa read under Rule 110 that a multi-storeyed commercial complex would be constructed at the South Asian Games Village in Koyambedu, Chennai, at an estimated cost of ₹116 crore. Where is that commercial complex?

She also announced that 120 additional homes would be built at the same place at a cost of ₹33 crore. Many months after the announcement, a Government Order was issued on 5 March 2012, and that was all. Funds have not been allotted, tenders called, work awarded, or the homes constructed.

On 8 September 2011, Jayalalithaa further announced that a seventeen-storey office complex, covering two lakh square feet and incorporating green-building features, would be built for approximately ₹100 crore on vacant land held by the Tamil Nadu Slum Clearance Board. The office complex has not appeared. There is no indication that even a Government Order was issued. It ended with the announcement!

The total estimated cost of the schemes Jayalalithaa announced under Rule 110 in 2011 was ₹5,423 crore. Only ₹970 crore was spent. That is 17.89 percent, which shows the limited extent to which those schemes took practical shape.

In a lengthy statement read under Rule 110 on 29 March 2012, Chief Minister Jayalalithaa announced one scheme: “I take great pleasure in informing this House that the Government under my leadership has now decided to implement a new project called the 660-megawatt Ennore Thermal Power Expansion Project. This thermal power project, to be started at an estimated cost of approximately ₹3,960 crore, will begin generation by the end of 2015.” Jayalalithaa made this announcement in the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly in 2012 amid great fanfare. Three and a half years have rolled by. As she said, the end of 2015 has arrived. Against the announced ₹3,960 crore, only ₹122 crore had been released for the project. Has power generation begun? Do you know the project's status as of 15 December 2014? “Preliminary construction work for the project has begun. The project is expected to become operational in 2017-18.” That is all! What an announcement! What lightning-fast (?) execution! While power cuts continue to torment the State, do you not understand the priority given to this power-expansion project, and the motive behind it?

On 7 May 2012, Jayalalithaa announced under Rule 110 a ₹1,560-crore project to improve drainage facilities in the Cauvery delta, declaring at length in the Assembly that about four and a half lakh acres of irrigated land would benefit. What happened? What is the status of the drainage-improvement work? The first-stage task of selecting experts to provide consultancy is reportedly only now underway. They say a contract will soon be concluded and action taken to begin the work. That is the speed shown even towards an announcement for farmers!

On 11 May 2012 came another announcement. Jayalalithaa told the Assembly: “With the aim of ensuring that heritage court buildings retain their antiquity while gaining renewed splendour, I am pleased to announce that maintenance work costing ₹22 crore will be undertaken on the historic George Town Court, Saidapet Court and Egmore Courts operating in Chennai, as well as the Small Causes Court building in the Madras High Court campus.” What do they say now? Renovation can begin only after alternative premises are found for those courts. Did that thought not occur before the announcement? A Government Order was issued only on 25 November 2014 to spend the amount on other courts.

Another grand announcement by Chief Minister Jayalalithaa on that same 11 May 2012 was that a memorandum of understanding worth ₹4,000 crore had been signed with GAIL and that three projects would be implemented through it. Three years have passed. What is the position now? The case concerning the project is pending before the Supreme Court. That is all!

On the same day, she happily announced that a 500-megawatt gas-based power project would be undertaken at a cost of ₹2,000 crore and later expanded with investment reaching ₹10,000 crore. The case concerning this project, too, is pending before the Supreme Court. What action has been taken to expedite that case and obtain a judgment for this important power-generation project?

The estimated cost of the schemes announced in 2012 alone was ₹18,188.81 crore. The allocation was ₹10,025.49 crore; ₹5,083.33 crore was released; but only ₹5,004.48 crore was spent. Of even that amount, ₹4,294.16 crore was a subsidy to the Electricity Board. Deducting it, only ₹710.32 crore was spent on schemes announced under Rule 110 in 2012! There is a tale about something wearing away until it became a tiny ant. Is this that very tale?

Let us turn to 2013.

On 1 April 2013, Jayalalithaa happily announced that sixty-seven government roads extending 215 kilometres would be laid at an estimated cost of ₹100 crore. Do you know the status on 15 December 2014? Action was reportedly being taken to obtain financial assistance from the National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development in 2015-16!

Another announcement that day said eighteen railway overbridges would be built in eleven districts at an estimated cost of ₹948.30 crore. “Approval from the Railways is awaited” is the fate of that announcement.

There was another announcement that day: an integrated satellite township would be created near Madurai Airport with 19,500 plots. Of them, 14,300 would be allotted to low-income groups, 2,500 to middle-income groups, and 1,950 to economically weaker sections. Roads, drinking-water supply, sewerage, street lights, rainwater harvesting, and parks would be provided at an estimated cost of ₹120 crore. For the welfare of its residents, every facility would be created, including a school campus, commercial plots, police station, post office, primary healthcare facility, fire station, and developed industrial plots. Jayalalithaa announced all this in the Assembly amid thunderous fanfare. A Government Order for the scheme announced on 4 April 2013 was issued six months later, on 21 October 2013, and tenders were invited. What is the situation now? Only the Almighty knows!

Likewise, on 4 April 2013, Jayalalithaa read under Rule 110 that 1,500 multi-storeyed homes would be built in Sholinganallur at an estimated cost of ₹612 crore. According to information dated 15 December 2014, work had supposedly begun. Of the total ₹612-crore project, the amount spent by then was a mere ₹15 lakh! It appears that in twenty months they had completed only the work of measuring the land and planting boundary stones.

Jayalalithaa announced under Rule 110 that on Housing Board land in nine districts, Chennai, Kancheepuram, Coimbatore, Erode, Krishnagiri, Salem, Virudhunagar, Dindigul, and Tiruchi, 1,630 individual houses and 2,192 apartments would be constructed at an estimated cost of ₹918.45 crore. Only ₹56.29 crore had been spent on this ₹918-crore scheme by 15 December 2014.

An announcement read on 10 April 2013 said combined drinking-water schemes worth ₹797.69 crore would be taken up for implementation during the year. ₹544.39 crore was allotted, but only ₹91.85 crore was released and spent. That, too, is the fate of the drinking-water schemes!

On 10 April 2013, Jayalalithaa read that drinking-water improvement schemes worth ₹227.26 crore would be undertaken in Tirunelveli Corporation and the municipalities of Arani, Tiruvathipuram, and Periyakulam. They said tenders had only then been invited. Drinking-water scarcity is rampant. Mothers wait with empty pots. The AIADMK Government has utterly failed to provide water to quench thirst!

On 25 April 2013 came enormous announcements. A 2,000-megawatt pumped-storage hydroelectric project at Sillahalla in the Nilgiris district would be established for ₹7,000 crore. What was its status two and a half years later? What indifference and neglect towards power-generation schemes! A detailed project report for constructing the dam was only then said to be under examination. The Government's term will end before the examination does!

They announced a ₹3,572-crore project with Japanese financial assistance to ensure stable power distribution. Tenders were only then being finalised. They announced that fifty-six substations would be established for ₹8,000 crore. Only ₹763 crore was spent.

The total estimated value of the schemes announced in 2013 was ₹37,720.69 crore. The amount spent was ₹6,488.76 crore, only 17.20 percent.

Now take 2014. It was announced that a new building for the Government Press would be constructed at an estimated cost of ₹21.59 crore. Preliminary work was said to have begun.

They announced that the Slum Clearance Board would construct ten thousand apartments for ₹825 crore. What was the position on 15 December 2014? Work towards issuing a Government Order was reportedly underway. They said 534 homes would be built at Nochikuppam for ₹48 crore. Issuing the Government Order was supposedly under consideration. Perhaps they were searching for paper on which to issue it? They said a multi-storeyed office building would be built at Koyambedu for ₹63.25 crore. Steps such as inviting tenders were reportedly being taken.

They read that a medical park would be established near Chengalpattu for ₹130 crore. A detailed project report had reportedly been requested. On 24 July 2014, Jayalalithaa announced in the Assembly that a National Investment and Manufacturing Zone would be established in the Ramanathapuram and Tiruvadanai taluks at an estimated cost of approximately ₹6,525 crore. Land acquisition was expected to be completed only by the end of 2016. In the same statement, they said a seawater-desalination project would be established at Valinokkam for ₹1,500 crore. Nothing had happened there either. They said an electronics-manufacturing project would be established in Krishnagiri district at an estimated cost of ₹920 crore. Approval from the Central and State Governments was reportedly awaited.

On 30 July 2014, Jayalalithaa announced: “Since maintaining clean toilets is essential for children's health, for the first time ₹160.77 crore will be allotted to maintain toilets. Through this, 56.55 lakh students will benefit.” The file was said to have been sent to the Finance Department on 25 November 2014. Has it returned? Is this such a formidable task, like Kanaka and Vijaya carrying a stone?

On 31 July 2014, Chief Minister Jayalalithaa read in the Assembly that, to improve Chennai's electricity infrastructure, two substations would be established for ₹338.08 crore, a 400-kilovolt substation at Kamuthi for ₹435.50 crore, and a substation at Virudhunagar for ₹47.51 crore. According to details dated 15 December 2014, tenders had been invited for some, while for others they were still going to be invited.

In another statement read under Rule 110 on 31 July 2014, she announced: “During this financial year, 28,000 distribution transformers will be procured and installed at a cost of ₹660 crore.” As of 15 December 2014, purchase orders were to be issued after draft purchase orders were approved. That same day, they said two polytechnic colleges would be started in Cuddalore and Tiruvallur districts for ₹24.60 crore. But on 15 December 2014, they said steps were still being taken to issue the Government Order.

On 4 August 2014, Jayalalithaa announced that three new fish-landing centres would be established for ₹17.15 crore; government fish-seed farms at four locations would be improved for ₹17.55 crore; and the Tamil Nadu Cooperative Milk Producers' Federation would take measures costing ₹46.50 crore to promote the use of cattle feed. But according to information dated 15 December 2014, the file seeking an order had been sent to the Chief Minister. The Chief Minister must explain what became of it.

The same day, they said the Public Works Department would implement agricultural-production schemes costing ₹670 crore. Of ten announcements, estimates had been prepared for only three, worth ₹86 crore, and sent to the Finance Department for approval. The Government must explain whether the Finance Department granted approval. Work to prepare estimates for the other seven announcements was reportedly only then underway.

On 7 August 2014, an announcement was read that two Commercial Taxes offices would be built for ₹60 crore. That same day, they said an integrated check-post would be established at Hosur for ₹120 crore. They announced that five integrated Registration Department complexes containing ten Sub-Registrar offices, along with twenty-five separate Sub-Registrar office buildings, would be built for ₹25 crore. These were said to be receiving the Government's swift attention and orders would soon be issued. Were the orders issued? The Chief Minister, who so often says, “I have ordered it, I have ordered it,” must answer.

They read an announcement that the “Amma Farm Women's Skill Development Scheme” would be implemented for ₹113.26 crore. They merely say that appropriate action is being taken, without saying what that action is.

In 2014 alone, schemes worth ₹23,042 crore were announced. Yet expenditure was incurred only on schemes worth ₹271.92 crore. That is just 1.18 percent.

Taking the four years together, the total estimated cost of the announcements read by the Chief Minister under Rule 110 was ₹84,374 crore. The amount spent was only ₹12,733.60 crore, just 12.09 percent! Under Rule 110, what the Chief Minister said was ocean-sized; what she did was only hand-sized! That is the fate of the statements read under Rule 110. The Tamil people must listen to all this; that is their fate!

With affection,

M.K.

26 August 2015

## Original Tamil

# 3975. கடுகளவும் நிறைவேற்றாத; கடல் அளவு அறிவிப்புகள்!



உடன்பிறப்பே,

சட்டப்பேரவை தொடங்கிவிட்டது. இனி ஒவ்வொரு நாளும் முதல் அமைச்சர் ஜெயலலிதா, பேரவை விதி 110இன் கீழ் அறிக்கைகளைப் படிக்கத் தொடங்கிவிடுவார். தமிழ்நாடு சட்டமன்றப் பேரவை விதி 110 என்ன கூறுகிறது என்றால், “பொது முக்கியத்துவம் வாய்ந்த ஒரு பொருளைப்பற்றி ஓர் அமைச்சர் அறிக்கை ஒன்றை அளிக்கலாம்” என்றுதான் உள்ளது. ஆனால் அந்த விதியில் உள்ள “ஓர் அமைச்சர்” என்பதை மாற்றி “முதலமைச்சர் மட்டுமே” என்று திரித்துப் பொருள்கொண்டு, கடந்த நான்காண்டு காலத்தில் எந்தத் துறை அமைச்சருக்கும் வாய்ப்பே கொடுக்காமல், முதலமைச்சர் ஒருவர் மட்டுமே தமிழகச் சட்டப்பேரவையில் 110வது விதியின்கீழ் அத்தனை அறிக்கைகளையும் படித்துக்கொண்டிருக்கிறார். இந்த நிலையில் கடந்த நான்காண்டு காலத்தில் ஜெயலலிதா 110வது விதியின்கீழ்ப் படித்த அறிக்கைகளிலே கூறப்பட்ட திட்டங்கள் என்னவாயின? நிறைவேறிவிட்டனவா? அல்லது ஏட்டளவிலேயே நின்றுபோய் விட்டனவா?

2011ஆம் ஆண்டு 8-6-2011 முதல் 13-9-2011 அன்று வரை முதலமைச்சர் ஜெயலலிதா 110வது விதியின்கீழ் 19 அறிக்கைகளைப்படித்தார். அந்த 19 அறிக்கைகளிலும் 54 அறிவிப்புகள் இருந்தன.

2012ஆம் ஆண்டு 29-3-2012 முதல் 2-11-2012 அன்று வரை முதலமைச்சர் ஜெயலலிதா 61 அறிக்கைகளைப் படித்தார். இந்த 61 அறிக்கைகளில் 99 அறிவிப்புகள் இடம்பெற்றிருந்தன. 2013ஆம் ஆண்டு 1-4-2013 முதல் 15-5-2013 அன்றுவரை முதலமைச்சர் ஜெயலலிதா 46 அறிக்கைகளைப் படித்திருக்கிறார். இந்த 46 அறிக்கைகளில் 189 அறிவிப்புகள் இடம்பெற்றிருந்தன.

2014ஆம் ஆண்டு 14-7-2014 முதல் 12-8-2014 அன்று வரை முதலமைச்சர் 39 அறிக்கைகளைப் படித்திருக்கிறார். இந்த 39 அறிக்கைகளில் 235 அறிவிப்புகள் இடம்பெற்றிருந்தன.

எனக்குக் கிடைத்திருக்கும் தகவல்படி, அதாவது முதலமைச்சர் ஜெயலலிதாவினால் 110 விதியின்கீழ் அறிவிக்கப்பட்ட எந்தெந்தத் திட்டங்கள் நிறைவேற்றப்படாமல் எஞ்சி நிற்கின்றன என்பதற்கான விவரத்தைத் தெரிவிக்கின்றேன்.

கோடியே 60 லட்ச ரூபாய் செலவில் தொடங்கப்படும் என்று கூறினார்கள். ஆனால் அதற்கான அரசாணை வெளியிட நடவடிக்கை மேற்கொள்ளப்பட்டு வருவதாக 15-12-2014இல் கூறுகிறார்கள்.

17 கோடியே. 15 லட்சம் ரூபாய் மதிப்பீட்டில் 3 புதிய மீன் இறங்கு தளங்கள் அமைக்கப்படும் என்றும், 17 கோடியே 55 லட்சம் ரூபாய் செலவில் நான்கு இடங்களில் உள்ள அரசு மீன் குஞ்சு வளர்ப்புப் பண்ணைகள் மேம்படுத்தப்படும் என்றும், தமிழ்நாடு பால் உற்பத்தியாளர் கூட்டுறவு இணையத்தின் சார்பில் 46 கோடியே 50 லட்சம் ரூபாய்ச் செலவில் கால்நடைத் தீவனப் பயன்பாட்டை ஊக்கப்படுத்த நடவடிக்கை மேற்கொள்ளப்படும் என்றும் 4-8-2014 அன்று ஜெயலலிதா அறிவித்தார். ஆனால் 15- 12-2014 தகவல்படி இதற்கான ஆணை வேண்டி முதலமைச்சர் அவர்களுக்குக் கோப்பு அனுப்பப்பட்டுள்ளதாகக் கூறப்பட்டது. அது என்னவானது என்பதை முதலமைச்சர்தான் விளக்க வேண்டும். அதே நாளில் பொதுப்பணித் துறை சார்பில் 670 கோடி ரூபாய்ச் செலவில் வேளாண் உற்பத்திக்கான திட்டங்களைச் செயல்படுத்தப் போவதாகக் கூறினார்கள். அதில் மொத்தம் 10 அறிவிப்புகளில், 86 கோடி ரூபாய்க்கான 3 அறிவிப்புகளுக்கு மட்டும் மதிப்பீடுகள் தயாரிக்கப்பட்டு நிதித் துறையின் ஒப்புதலுக்காக அனுப்பப்பட்டுள்ளதாகத் தெரிவிக்கப்பட்டது. நிதித் துறை ஒப்புதல் அளித்துவிட்டதா என்பதை அரசுதான் விளக்க வேண்டும். இதர 7 அறிவிப்புகளுக்கு மதிப்பீடுகள் தயாரிக்கும் பணி தற்போதுதான் நடைபெற்று வருவதாகக் கூறப்படுகிறது.

7-8-2014 அன்று இரண்டு வணிக வரி அலுவலகங்கள் 60 கோடி ரூபாய்ச் செலவில் கட்டப்படும் என்ற அறிவிப்பு படிக்கப்பட்டது. அதே நாளில், 120 கோடி ரூபாய்ச் செலவில் ஒருங்கிணைந்த சோதனைச் சாவடி ஒன்று ஓசூரில் நிறுவப்படும் என்றார்கள். 25 கோடி ரூபாய்ச் செலவில் பத்து சார்பதிவாளர் அலுவலகங்களை உள்ளடக்கிய 5 ஒருங்கிணைந்த பதிவுத் துறை வளாகங்கள் மற்றும் தனித்தனியாக 25 சார்பதிவாளர் அலுவலகக் கட்டடங்கள் கட்டப்படும் என்றெல்லாம் அறிவித்தார்கள். இவை அரசின் துரித நடவடிக்கையில் உள்ளதாகவும், விரைவில் ஆணைகள் பிறப்பிக்கப்படும் என்று கூறப்பட்டது. ஆணை பிறப்பிக்கப்பட்டு விட்டதா என்ன? “ஆணையிட்டுள்ளேன், ஆணையிட்டுள்ளேன்” என்று அடிக்கடி கூறும் முதலமைச்சர்தான் பதில் கூற வேண்டும்.

113 கோடியே 26 லட்ச ரூபாய்ச் செலவில் “அம்மா பண்ணை மகளிர் திறன் மேம்பாட்டுத் திட்டம்” செயல்படுத்தப்படும் என்று அறிக்கை படித்தார்கள். உரிய நடவடிக்கை மேற்கொள்ளப்பட்டு வருவதாகக் கூறப்படுகிறதே தவிர, என்ன நடவடிக்கை என்று தெரியவில்லை.

2014ஆம் ஆண்டில் மட்டும் 23 ஆயிரத்து 42 கோடி ரூபாய்க்கான திட்டங்கள் அறிவிக்கப்பட்டன. அதில் செலவு செய்யப்பட்டது என்னவோ 271.92 கோடி ரூபாய்க்கான திட்டங்களுக்குத்தான். அதாவது 1.18 சதவிகிதம்தான்.
